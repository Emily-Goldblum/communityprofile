Shifting Perceptions Towards Acceptance of LGBT People

Acceptance of same-sex marriage and rights for LGBT people is growing nationwide; through marriage and anti-discrimination laws. While there has been major headway towards normalizing ideas the LGBT community within our society, many steps still need to be taken. Anti-gay organizations are working twice as hard to slow this progress down - even in states where same-sex marriage has already been legalized.

By Emily Goldblum & Betsy Laikin | @emilygoldblum @betsylaikin | March 20, 2015

Source: USNews

Introduction

Acceptance of Gays on a Continual Rise

Source: Pew Research Center

Every year acceptance is growing among women and men in the United States. According to Pew Research Center, there has been a significant increase in acceptance even in the past five years.

Acceptance of same-sex couples is growing nationwide. Thirty-seven states have legalized same-sex marriage, and married gay couples are listed as “married” in every state, regardless of the bans the state may have against it. This could mean the couples have been married in other states and returned/moved to these states. The New York Times posted an article in Upshot about most popular and least popular metro areas for people who identify as LGBT. Unsurprisingly, San Francisco has the highest percentage, with 6.2% of its residents in this demographic, while Birmingham, Alabama has the lowest rate of LGBT residents. One noteworthy statistic is that 4.2% of Ohio’s population population are LGBT, despite same-sex marriage being banned their state law. (We would like to survey those in Ohio to see why so many LGBT people reside in a state that doesn't view their marriage as legal.) In 2003, a bill was passed called Ohio's Defense of Marriage Act. In May of 2004, the Sixth Circuit ruled that the Ohio's ban on same-sex marriage did not violate the constitution. Also, in Louisville, KY, 4.7% of city dwellers are LGBT residents. In 2014, U.S. District Judge John G. Heyburn II attempted to overturn the gay marriage ban in Kentucky, but it was overruled by a three-judge panel of the Sixth US Circuit Court of Appeals.

Who Should Know?

With the continuing increase in acceptance of same-sex marriage, and visibility of the LGBT community in media, people are feeling confident about exploring their sexuality, instead of suppressing their emotions. The growth of the LGBT community in studies doesn't necessarily mean there are more queer people today; but that people are more comfortable with revealing their sexuality. **Search for Data that Compares Coming out in 90s compared to chart below.**

Discrepancies in Age Between When Gay Men, Lesbians, and Bisexuals Come Out

The median age for coming out and being confident of your sexual orientation were pretty even across the board for all categories polled. Most people polled start to think they knew of of their sexual orientation by their early teens, told someone as teenagers, and were confident with their sexual identities by their late teens or early twenties. Overall, gay men were most comfortable about coming out at the youngest ages, and lesbians tended to wait a few years longer than the others.

Who's Against Them?

While acceptance of marriage for same-sex couples is on the rise, there are a number of detractors attempting to ban or withhold bans against same-sex marriages in their states. Three such organizations are the powerful anti-gay organizations; The Family Research Council, the American Family Association and Focus on the Family.

Focus on the Family claims to "Help Families Thrive" and are adamant that marriage should be between husband and wife. They raised over $515 million from 2008 - 2012 alone making them the most well-funded anti-LGBT organization in America. They released an article regarding the amount of gays and lesbians in the U.S. The writer of the article, Jeff Johnson, feels that the polls by Gallup from 2011 are false. "I think the reason most people overestimate their numbers are because gay- and lesbian-identified people have done a terrific job of promoting their agenda over the past 40 years. This group is highly visible and wields much influence," Johnson said. He makes it clear that the population is much smaller than proved to be. The President of Focus on the Family, Jim Daley mentions marriage equality means the destruction of civilization. Since leaders of the anti-gay movement are saying such vile remarks about the LGBT community this helps push those who believe them to help fight back against the rights of LGBT people. They work hand in hand with other national organizations to point out where these LGBT safe spaces are.

It’s ironic that the virulently anti-gay American Family Association named their interactive project of locating LGBT, Humanist and Atheist organizations the “Bigotry Map.” LGBT organizations are described as “Advocates for the legalization and promotion of same-sex marriage and viciously attacks Christians who exercise their First Amendment right to voice support for God’s plan for marriage as between one man and one woman.” On the East coast, it mainly maps out locations of state chapters of the national organization Gay, Lesbian & Straight Education Network, known as GLSEN, as well as the Human Rights Campaign, or HRC. Both organizations tirelessly work to achieve equality for the LGBT community.

What's in the works?

There are international, country and state organizations that are fighting for same-sex couples and LGBT to have the same rights as heterosexual people. Every state in the U.S. has one or more organizations fighting for these rights. Without counting statewide organizations there are currently just under 80 orgs with the goal of equality. These numbers are growing. While there are older coalitions such as Act Up that focus specifically on the HIV/AIDs movement, there are organizations like GetEqual that was formed in 2010 taking bold action towards demanding full equality, legally and socially.
